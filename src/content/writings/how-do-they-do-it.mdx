In high school, I was diagnosed with sleep apnea.

It took me hours to fall asleep. I caught up on sleep in History.

I was also living on potato soup. Sometimes pudding.

In my French homework, I listed my favorite foods as potatoes, milk, and butter. These were not foods, but a recipe.

To my mother, I was disabled. I thought I was disabled, too.

For the sleep study, they hooked me up to a bunch of machines and asked that I lie down. I hated lying down; I slept sitting up due to pain from chronic constipation (I was living on potato soup).

I slipped out of REM sleep sixty-six times that night. We had a verdict: I was disabled. I was prescribed sleep patches to help me stay awake in history.

The skin patches irritated my dry skin (I was living on potato soup). I didn't use them for long. I kept falling asleep in History.

In college, it remained one of my worst subjects.

I remained disabled.
